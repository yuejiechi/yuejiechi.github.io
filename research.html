Yuejie Chi

Motivated by the challenge of harnessing actionable information from large-scale and high-dimensional data in sample-starved, resource-constrained, complex and uncertain environments, I am interested in revealing and exploiting low-dimensional data representations and problem structures to improve resource efficiency in terms of statistical, computational, and communication complexities and achieve their optimal trade-offs. My group develops theoretical understandings and provable algorithms for applications broadly spanning generative AI, imaging science, and sensing systems across scientific and engineering domains.

Some (intertwined) lines of research include:

  • foundations and applications of generative AI;

  • statistical and algorithmic foundations of reinforcement learning;

  • nonconvex optimization for high-dimensional statistical learning and estimation;

  • resource-efficient decentralized and federated learning;

  • inverse problems and super-resolution imaging;

  • signal processing under geometric constraints for streaming, incomplete, and messy data;

with applications in sensing, imaging, networks, healthcare, and more.
